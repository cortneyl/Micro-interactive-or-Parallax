What is a sunset?

A sunset is what you see when the Sun appears to go down below the horizon at the end of the day. Even though it looks like the Sun is moving, what’s really happening is that the Earth is rotating. As your location turns away from the Sun, it gradually disappears from view—that moment is the sunset.

As the Sun sets, the sky often changes into a mix of beautiful colors like orange, pink, red, and purple. This happens because of a process called Rayleigh scattering, where sunlight passes through more of Earth’s atmosphere at a low angle. Shorter wavelengths of light, like blue, are scattered away, while longer wavelengths like red and orange remain visible. This is why sunsets can look so vibrant and different each day depending on the weather and amount of particles in the air.

Fun Facts

  • No two sunsets are exactly the same because the atmosphere is always changing.
  • Sunsets can appear more colorful when there is dust, pollution, or clouds in the air.
  • The Sun actually sets slightly after it appears to due to the bending of light, called Atmospheric refraction.
  • Planets like Mars also have sunsets however, they can look blue instead of red!
